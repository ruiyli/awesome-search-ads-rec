| 1 | [HiSAC: Hierarchical Sparse Activation Compression for Ultra-long Sequence Modeling in Recommenders](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.21009) | Alibaba | Kun Yuan, Junyu Bi, Daixuan Cheng, Changfa Wu, Shuwen Xiao, Binbin Cao, Jian Wu, Yuning Jiang | This paper addresses the high computational costs and memory constraints of modeling ultra-long user behavior sequences in recommender systems, specifically overcoming the quantization errors and loss of valuable long-tail preferences common in existing sequence compression methods. To solve these issues, the authors propose the **Hierarchical Sparse Activation Compression (HiSAC)** framework, which innovates by tokenizing historical behaviors into multi-level semantic identifiers, extracting personalized interest-agents via a hierarchical voting mechanism, and employing Soft-Routing Attention to accurately aggregate interaction signals based on semantic similarity. Deployed in an industrial setting, HiSAC successfully reduced end-to-end inference latency by over 40% while delivering substantial real-world performance gains, including a 1.65% increase in Click-Through Rate and a 2.56% increase in total orders during online A/B testing. | - |
| 2 | [Offline Reasoning for Efficient Recommendation: LLM-Empowered Persona-Profiled Item Indexing](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.21756) | Yonsei University | Deogyong Kim, Junseong Lee, Jeongeun Lee, Changhoe Kim, Junguel Lee, Jungseok Lee, Dongha Lee | This paper addresses the high inference latency and poor scalability of current LLM-based recommender systems, which typically rely on expensive online reasoning. To solve this, the authors propose **Persona4Rec**, an innovative framework that shifts LLM reasoning offline to construct multiple review-grounded user personas for each item, enabling real-time online recommendations through lightweight similarity scoring between user profiles and these pre-computed personas. Consequently, Persona4Rec achieves recommendation accuracy comparable to state-of-the-art LLM rerankers while reducing inference time by up to 99.6% and naturally providing human-interpretable explanations for its outputs. | [Persona4Rec](https://github.com/legenduck/PERSONA4REC) |
| 3 | [Turning Semantics into Topology: LLM-Driven Attribute Augmentation for Collaborative Filtering](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.21099) | University of Science and Technology of China | Junjie Meng, Ranxu zhang, Wei Wu, Rui Zhang, Chuan Qin, Qi Zhang, Qi Liu, Hui Xiong, Chao Wang | This paper addresses the challenge of effectively integrating LLMs' semantic knowledge into collaborative filtering without incurring the prohibitive computational costs of direct inference or the dimension mismatches of textual embedding augmentation. To solve this, the authors propose **Topology-Augmented Graph Collaborative Filtering (TAGCF)**, which innovates by utilizing LLMs to extract interaction intents as intermediate attribute nodes within an enriched User-Attribute-Item graph and employing Adaptive Relation-weighted Graph Convolution (ARGC) to dynamically aggregate these heterogeneous structural signals. Experimental results demonstrate that TAGCF consistently achieves state-of-the-art recommendation performance across multiple backbones and datasets, while successfully mitigating data sparsity, cold-start issues, and the over-smoothing problem common in deep graph networks. | [TAGCF](https://anonymous.4open.science/r/AGCF-2441353190/README.md) |
| 4 | [Generative Pseudo-Labeling for Pre-Ranking with LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.20995) | Alibaba | Junyu Bi, Xinting Niu, Daixuan Cheng, Kun Yuan, Tao Wang, Binbin Cao, Jian Wu, Yuning Jiang | This paper addresses the severe sample selection bias in pre-ranking recommender systems—where models trained only on exposed items struggle to generalize to unexposed candidates—by proposing the **Generative Pseudo-Labeling (GPL)** framework. GPL innovates by leveraging large language models to offline generate user-specific interest anchors within a frozen multimodal semantic space, producing uncertainty-calibrated pseudo-labels for unexposed items without adding online serving latency. Deployed in a large-scale industrial setting, GPL successfully increased the click-through rate by 3.07% and the click-through conversion rate by 2.51% while significantly enhancing recommendation diversity and long-tail item discovery. | - |
| 5 | [ManCAR: Manifold-Constrained Latent Reasoning with Adaptive Test-Time Computation for Sequential Recommendation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.20093) | Xiamen University | Kun Yang, Yuxuan Zhu, Yazhe Chen, Siyao Zheng, Bangyang Hong, Kangle Wu, Yabo Ni, Anxiang Zeng, Cong Fu, Hui Li | This paper addresses the issue of "latent drift" in current latent reasoning approaches for sequential recommendation, where unconstrained intermediate reasoning states often deviate into implausible regions and degrade model robustness. To solve this, the authors propose **ManCAR**, an innovative framework that restricts latent reasoning trajectories within a valid "collaborative manifold" defined by a global interaction graph, utilizing a graph-conditioned teacher prior and a convergence-based adaptive stopping mechanism at test time. Experiments across seven benchmark datasets demonstrate that ManCAR consistently outperforms state-of-the-art baselines, achieving up to a 46.88% relative improvement in NDCG@10 while enabling highly stable and efficient data-aware inference. | [ManCAR](https://github.com/FuCongResearchSquad/ManCAR) |
| 6 | [Bending the Scaling Law Curve in Large-Scale Recommendation Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16986) | Meta | Qin Ding, Kevin Course, Linjian Ma, Jianhui Sun, Ruochen Liu, Zhao Zhu, Chunxing Yin, Wei Li, Dai Li, Yu Shi, Xuan Cao, Ze Yang, Han Li, Xing Liu, Bi Xue, Hongwei Li, Rui Jian, Daisy Shi He, Jing Qian, Matt Ma, Qunshu Zhang, Rui Li | This paper addresses the quadratic computational bottleneck of modeling ultra-long user histories in large-scale recommender systems, where traditional self-attention becomes impractical and existing cross-attention workarounds severely limit representational power. To overcome this, the authors propose **ULTRA-HSTU**, which innovates through an end-to-end model and system co-design featuring a linear-complexity Semi-Local Attention mechanism, hardware-aware mixed-precision optimizations, and dynamic topological structures like Attention Truncation. Deployed in a production environment serving billions of users, ULTRA-HSTU achieved over 5x faster training and 21x faster inference scaling efficiencies, successfully driving substantial 4% to 8% improvements in real-world consumption and engagement metrics. | - |
| 7 | [PRECTR-V2:Unified Relevance-CTR Framework with Cross-User Preference Mining, Exposure Bias Correction, and LLM-Distilled Encoder Optimization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.20676) | Alibaba | Shuzhi Cao, Rong Chen, Ailong He, Shuguang Han, Jufeng Chen | This paper addresses the limitations of unified search relevance and CTR prediction models, specifically their ineffective personalization for cold-start users, training generalization issues caused by exposure bias, and the architectural misalignment of using frozen text encoders. To solve these challenges, the authors propose **PRECTR-V2**, which innovates by utilizing cross-user behavior mining to extract global relevance preferences, applying synthetic hard negative sampling with a pairwise ranking loss to correct exposure bias, and introducing an LLM-distilled lightweight encoder for end-to-end joint optimization. Deployed on a large-scale e-commerce platform, PRECTR-V2 significantly outperformed baseline models offline and successfully delivered a 1.39% increase in per capita orders alongside a 3.18% boost in Gross Merchandise Volume (GMV) during online A/B testing. | - |
| 8 | [EST: Towards Efficient Scaling Laws in Click-Through Rate Prediction via Unified Modeling](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.10811) | Alibaba | Mingyang Liu, Yong Bai, Zhangming Chan, Sishuo Chen, Xiang-Rong Sheng, Han Zhu, Jian Xu, Xinyang Chen | This paper addresses the challenge of efficiently scaling industrial CTR prediction models under strict latency constraints, where existing methods suffer from information loss due to the early aggregation or partially unified modeling of long, heterogeneous feature sequences. To overcome this, the authors propose the **Efficiently Scalable Transformer (EST)**, which innovates by organizing all raw inputs into a single fully unified sequence and employing Lightweight Cross-Attention (LCA) to prioritize critical cross-feature interactions alongside Content Sparse Attention (CSA) to efficiently integrate multimodal semantic signals. Deployed on Taobao's large-scale advertising platform, EST established a highly stable power-law scaling relationship and successfully delivered substantial real-world gains, including a 3.27% increase in Revenue Per Mile (RPM) and a 1.22% lift in CTR. | - |
| 9 | [CADET: Context-Conditioned Ads CTR Prediction With a Decoder-Only Transformer](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.11410) | LinkedIn | David Pardoe, Neil Daftary, Miro Furtado, Aditya Aiyer, Yu Wang, Liuqing Li, Tao Song, Lars Hertel, Young Jin Yun, Senthil Radhakrishnan, Zhiwei Wang, Tommy Li, Khai Tran, Ananth Nagarajan, Ali Naqvi, Yue Zhang, Renpeng Fang, Avi Romascanu, Arjun Kulothungun, Deepak Kumar, Praneeth Boda, Fedor Borisyuk, Ruoyan Wang | This paper addresses the challenges of adapting decoder-only transformers for industrial-scale ads CTR prediction, specifically the issues of handling post-scoring contextual signals like ad position, maintaining offline-online consistency, and scaling to production workloads. To overcome these, the authors propose the **CADET** framework, which innovates through a context-conditioned multi-tower decoding architecture to resolve ranking dependencies, a self-gated attention mechanism for training stability, timestamp-based temporal encoding, session-aware masking, and custom production engineering optimizations like sequence packing and tailored Flash Attention kernels. Deployed on LinkedIn's advertising platform, CADET successfully served main traffic under strict latency constraints and achieved a substantial 11.04% increase in CTR along with a 0.14% revenue lift during online A/B testing. | - |
| 10 | [RQ-GMM: Residual Quantized Gaussian Mixture Model for Multimodal Semantic Discretization in CTR Prediction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.12593) | Tencent | Ziye Tong, Jiahao Liu, Weimin Zhang, Hongji Ruan, Derick Tang, Zhanpeng Zeng, Qinsong Zeng, Peng Zhang, Tun Lu, Ning Gu| This paper addresses the suboptimal performance of existing multimodal semantic discretization methods in CTR prediction, which struggle with limited codebook utilization, training dynamics mismatches, and poor boundary sample reconstruction. To overcome these challenges, the authors propose the Residual **Quantized Gaussian Mixture Model (RQ-GMM)**, an innovative framework that combines residual quantization with probabilistic Gaussian modeling and soft assignments to accurately capture the complex statistical structures of multimodal embedding spaces. Deployed on a large-scale short-video platform serving hundreds of millions of users, RQ-GMM successfully achieved near-complete offline codebook utilization and delivered a significant 1.502% improvement in Advertiser Value during online A/B testing. | - |
| 11 | [From Noise to Order: Learning to Rank via Denoising Diffusion](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.11453) | University of Guelph | Sajad Ebrahimi, Bhaskar Mitra, Negar Arabzadeh, Ye Yuan, Haolun Wu, Fattane Zarrinkalam, Ebrahim Bagheri | This paper addresses the limitation of traditional Learning-to-Rank methods that rely solely on discriminative machine learning, which can lead to over-parameterized models that lack robustness when fitting training data. To solve this, the authors propose **DiffusionRank**, an innovative deep generative framework that utilizes a denoising diffusion process to jointly model the full distribution of tabular feature vectors and discrete relevance labels for both pointwise and pairwise objectives. Empirical results on benchmark datasets demonstrate that DiffusionRank achieves statistically significant improvements in ranking effectiveness over discriminative baselines, offering more stable training dynamics and enhanced robustness against overfitting. | [DiffusionRank](https://anonymous.4open.science/r/DiffusionRank-1E51/) |
| 12 | [MaRI: Accelerating Ranking Model Inference via Structural Re-parameterization in Large Scale Recommendation System](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.23105) | Kuaishou | Yusheng Huang, Pengbo Xu, Shen Wang, Changxin Lao, Jiangxia Cao, Shuang Wen, Shuang Yang, Zhaojie Liu, Han Li, Kun Gai | This paper addresses the high inference latency in large-scale recommendation systems caused by redundant user-side matrix multiplication computations during feature fusion. To solve this, the authors propose **Matrix Re-parameterized Inference (MaRI)**, an innovative framework that structurally re-parameterizes MatMul to decouple user, item, and cross feature computations, utilizing a Graph Coloring Algorithm (GCA) for automated optimizable node detection and a feature reorganization strategy to prevent fragmented layout inefficiencies. Deployed on a real-world industrial platform, MaRI successfully achieved up to a 1.32x inference speedup and a 5.9% reduction in coarse-ranking hardware resources without any loss in recommendation accuracy. | - |
| 13 | [SIGMA: A Semantic-Grounded Instruction-Driven Generative Multi-Task Recommender at AliExpress](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.22913) | Alibaba | Yang Yu, Lei Kou, Huaikuan Yi, Bin Chen, Yayu Cao, Lei Shen, Chao Zhang, Bing Wang, Xiaoyi Zeng | This paper addresses the limitations of current generative recommenders, which typically focus only on next-item prediction and struggle to rapidly adapt to dynamic trends or satisfy diverse, task-specific business demands. | 14 | [Vectorizing the Trie: Efficient Constrained Decoding for LLM-based Generative Retrieval on Accelerators](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.22647) | YouTube | Zhengyang Su, Isay Katsman, Yueqi Wang, Ruining He, Lukasz Heldt, Raghunandan Keshavan, Shao-Chuan Wang, Xinyang Yi, Mingyan Gao, Onkar Dalal, Lichan Hong, Ed Chi, Ningren Han | This paper addresses the severe latency bottlenecks and hardware incompatibilities caused by using standard pointer-chasing prefix trees for constrained decoding in large-scale, LLM-based generative recommender systems. To solve this, the authors propose **STATIC**, an innovative framework that flattens dynamic prefix trees into static Compressed Sparse Row (CSR) matrices to execute fully vectorized, branch-free sparse matrix operations with $O(1)$ memory access overhead on modern hardware accelerators like TPUs and GPUs. Deployed on YouTube's massive video recommendation platform, STATIC achieved up to a 1033x speedup over existing baselines with near-zero latency overhead, successfully driving a 5.1% increase in fresh video views and a 0.15% lift in click-through rate during online A/B testing. | [STATIC](https://github.com/youtube/static-constraint-decoding) |
| 15 | [Fine-grained Semantics Integration for Large Language Model-based Recommendation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.22632) | University of Science and Technology of China | Jiawen Feng, Xiaoyu Kong, Leheng Sheng, Bin Wu, Chao Yi, Feifang Yang, Xiang-Rong Sheng, Han Zhu, Xiang Wang, Jiancan Wu, Xiangnan He | This paper addresses the limitations of current LLM-based generative recommenders, which struggle to effectively understand item Semantic Identifiers (SIDs) due to semantically meaningless token initialization and coarse-grained item-level alignment. To solve these issues, the authors propose **TS-Rec**, an innovative framework that integrates fine-grained semantics through Semantic-Aware Embedding Initialization (SA-Init) to inject semantic priors into SID tokens, and Token-level Semantic Alignment (TS-Align) to explicitly map individual tokens to their semantic meanings via multi-task tuning. Experiments on real-world datasets demonstrate that TS-Rec consistently outperforms existing baselines with up to a 7.17% relative improvement in ranking metrics, while also providing a superior, semantically grounded backbone that significantly elevates the performance ceiling of subsequent reinforcement learning optimization. | - |
| 16 | [From Agnostic to Specific: Latent Preference Diffusion for Multi-Behavior Sequential Recommendation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.23132) | University of Chinese Academy of Sciences | Ruochen Yang, Xiaodong Li, Jiawei Sheng, Jiangxia Cao, Xinkui Lin, Shen Wang, Shuang Yang, Zhaojie Liu, Tingwen Liu | This paper addresses the limitations of current multi-behavior sequential recommendation systems, which typically rely on discriminative paradigms that ignore underlying user intentions and struggle to generate diverse, behavior-specific predictions.
